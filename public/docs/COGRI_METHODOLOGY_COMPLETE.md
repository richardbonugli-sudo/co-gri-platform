## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 Overview

The Corporate Geopolitical Risk Index (CO-GRI) is a comprehensive quantitative framework for assessing multinational corporations' exposure to geopolitical risks. It combines geographic exposure data, country-level risk assessments, sector-specific factors, and political alignment considerations to produce a single risk score ranging from 0 (minimal risk) to 100 (maximum risk).

### 1.2 Key Features

- **Multi-Channel Analysis**: Four distinct exposure channels (Revenue, Supply Chain, Assets, Financial)
- **Country Shock Index (CSI)**: Standardized country risk scores (0-100)
- **Sector Multipliers**: Industry-specific risk adjustments
- **Political Alignment Factors**: Home country relationship considerations
- **Predictive Forecasting**: Forward-looking risk projections with Cedar Owl 2026 data
- **Scenario Analysis**: Base case, optimistic, and pessimistic projections
- **Time Horizons**: 6-month, 1-year, 2-year, and 5-year forecasts
- **Trading Signals**: Caldara-Iacoviello GPR and CO-GRI-based signals
- **Portfolio Analytics**: Comprehensive portfolio risk assessment
- **Expert Forecasts**: Geopolitical Investment Risk Gurus panel
- **CSI Propagation Network**: Event impact modeling across trade networks

### 1.3 Applications

- Investment risk assessment
- Portfolio diversification
- Strategic planning
- Supply chain risk management
- Regulatory compliance
- Stakeholder communication
- Trading signal generation
- Expert consensus forecasting

## 2. Theoretical Foundation

### 2.1 Conceptual Framework

CO-GRI is built on three theoretical pillars:

#### 2.1.1 Geographic Exposure Theory
Corporations face risks proportional to their geographic footprint across different markets and regulatory environments.

#### 2.1.2 Channel-Specific Risk Transmission
Geopolitical shocks transmit through distinct channels with varying intensities:
- **Revenue Channel**: Market access, demand shocks, trade barriers
- **Supply Channel**: Input disruptions, logistics constraints
- **Asset Channel**: Property rights, expropriation, capital controls
- **Financial Channel**: Currency risk, payment systems, sanctions

#### 2.1.3 Political Economy of International Business
Home country-host country political relationships significantly moderate risk exposure through diplomatic protection, trade agreements, and alliance structures.

### 2.2 Risk Decomposition

Total geopolitical risk decomposes into:

```
Total Risk = Σ(Channel Risk × Channel Weight)

Where:
Channel Risk = f(Geographic Exposure, Country Risk, Sector Factors, Political Alignment)
```

## 3. Core Methodology

### 3.1 Four-Channel Model

#### 3.1.1 Channel Definitions

| Channel | Weight | Components |
|---------|--------|------------|
| **Revenue** | 40% | Sales revenue by geographic market, customer concentration, market access |
| **Supply Chain** | 35% | Supplier locations, manufacturing facilities, critical inputs, logistics |
| **Asset** | 15% | Physical assets, intellectual property, investments, subsidiaries |
| **Financial** | 10% | Banking relationships, currency exposures, payment systems, capital access |

#### 3.1.2 Channel Weights Justification

Weights are derived from empirical analysis of how geopolitical shocks historically impact corporate performance:

- **Revenue (40%)**: Largest direct impact on cash flows
- **Supply (35%)**: Critical for operational continuity
- **Assets (15%)**: Longer-term strategic importance
- **Financial (10%)**: Diversifiable through hedging

### 3.2 Country Shock Index (CSI)

#### 3.2.1 CSI Components

| Component | Weight | Indicators |
|-----------|--------|------------|
| Political Stability | 30% | Government effectiveness, political violence, regime stability |
| Economic Conditions | 25% | GDP volatility, inflation, debt levels, currency stability |
| Regulatory Environment | 20% | Rule of law, property rights, contract enforcement |
| Geopolitical Tensions | 15% | Conflicts, territorial disputes, sanctions exposure |
| Social Factors | 10% | Social unrest, labor conditions, demographics |

#### 3.2.2 CSI Calculation Formula

```
CSI_country = Σ(Component_i × Weight_i)

Where:
Component_i = Normalized score (0-100) for component i
Weight_i = Component weight
```

#### 3.2.3 CSI Score Ranges

| Range | Risk Level | Description |
|-------|------------|-------------|
| 0-25 | Low Risk | Stable democracies, strong institutions |
| 25-40 | Moderate Risk | Emerging markets, some volatility |
| 40-60 | High Risk | Political instability, weak institutions |
| 60-100 | Very High Risk | Conflict zones, failed states |

## 4. Mathematical Framework

### 4.1 Core CO-GRI Formula

```
CO-GRI = [
  (Revenue_Channel × 0.40) +
  (Supply_Channel × 0.35) +
  (Asset_Channel × 0.15) +
  (Financial_Channel × 0.10)
] × Sector_Multiplier

Where each Channel is:
Channel = Σ(Exposure_% × CSI × Political_Alignment) / 100
```

### 4.2 Channel-Specific Calculations

#### 4.2.1 Revenue Channel Formula

```
Revenue_Channel = Σ(Revenue_j / Total_Revenue × CSI_j × Alignment_j)
```

**Worked Example:**
```
Company XYZ Revenue Distribution:
- US: $500M (50%), CSI: 35, Alignment: 1.00
- China: $300M (30%), CSI: 58, Alignment: 1.15
- Germany: $200M (20%), CSI: 28, Alignment: 0.85

Revenue_Channel = (0.50 × 35 × 1.00) + (0.30 × 58 × 1.15) + (0.20 × 28 × 0.85)
                = 17.5 + 20.01 + 4.76 = 42.27
```

#### 4.2.2 Supply Chain Channel Formula

```
Supply_Channel = Σ(Supply_j / Total_Supply × CSI_j × Alignment_j × Criticality_j)

Criticality Factors:
- Single source: 1.5
- Dual source: 1.2
- Multiple sources: 1.0
```

#### 4.2.3 Asset Channel Formula

```
Asset_Channel = Σ(Asset_Value_j / Total_Assets × CSI_j × Alignment_j × Asset_Type_j)

Asset Type Factors:
- Physical assets: 1.2
- Financial assets: 1.0
- IP/Intangible: 1.3
```

#### 4.2.4 Financial Channel Formula

```
Financial_Channel = Σ(Financial_Exposure_j × CSI_j × Alignment_j × Liquidity_j)

Liquidity Factor: 0.8 to 1.2
```

### 4.3 Complete Calculation Example

```
Given:
- Revenue_Channel = 42.27
- Supply_Channel = 61.96
- Asset_Channel = 35.50
- Financial_Channel = 28.00
- Sector = Technology (Multiplier = 1.12)

CO-GRI = [(42.27 × 0.40) + (61.96 × 0.35) + (35.50 × 0.15) + (28.00 × 0.10)] × 1.12
       = [16.91 + 21.69 + 5.33 + 2.80] × 1.12
       = 46.73 × 1.12
       = 52.34

Risk Level: High Risk
```

### 4.4 Confidence Intervals

```
CO-GRI_Confidence = ±(Data_Quality_Factor × Volatility_Factor)

Confidence Levels:
- High (±3 points): Complete data, stable countries, diversified exposure
- Moderate (±5 points): Good data, some volatility, moderate concentration
- Low (±8 points): Limited data, high volatility, concentrated exposure
```

## 9. Service 3: Assess a Portfolio

### 9.1 Overview

Calculates aggregate geopolitical risk for a portfolio including weighted average CO-GRI, diversification benefits, and concentration risk.

### 9.2 Portfolio CO-GRI Formula

```
Portfolio_COGRI = Σ(w_i × COGRI_i)

Where:
w_i = Market_Value_i / Total_Portfolio_Value
COGRI_i = CO-GRI score for holding i
```

### 9.3 Diversification Benefit

```
Diversification_Benefit = Weighted_Avg_Individual_COGRI - Portfolio_COGRI
```

### 9.4 Concentration Risk (Herfindahl Index)

```
HHI_Portfolio = Σ(w_i²) × 10,000

Interpretation:
- HHI < 1,500: Low Concentration (Well Diversified)
- HHI 1,500-2,500: Moderate Concentration
- HHI > 2,500: High Concentration
```

### 9.5 Geographic Concentration

```
HHI_Geographic = Σ(country_exposure_j²) × 10,000
```

### 9.6 Portfolio Optimization

```
Minimize: Portfolio_COGRI
Subject to:
    Σ(w_i) = 1
    w_i ≥ 0 for all i
    Expected_Return ≥ Target_Return
    Sector_Exposure_j ≤ Max_Sector_Weight
```

```python
def generate_gpr_signal(current_gpr, historical_gpr):
    gpr_mean = np.mean(historical_gpr[-252:])
    gpr_std = np.std(historical_gpr[-252:])
    z_score = (current_gpr - gpr_mean) / gpr_std
    momentum_5d = calculate_momentum(historical_gpr, 5)
    momentum_20d = calculate_momentum(historical_gpr, 20)
```

```python
    elif ma_3m[-1] < ma_12m[-1] - 5:
        return "Declining Risk"
    return "Stable"
```

```python
def detect_csi_correlations(countries):

    # Identify highly correlated country pairs
    high_correlations = [(i, j, correlations[i,j]) 
                         for i, j in combinations(range(len(countries)), 2)
                         if correlations[i,j] > 0.7]
    return high_correlations
```

## 18. Service 12: CSI Propagation Network

### 18.1 Overview

Models how geopolitical events propagate through trade networks and affect multiple countries.

### 18.2 Event Propagation Model

```
Propagated_Impact_j = Direct_Impact_j + Σ(Trade_Link_ij × Impact_i × Decay^hop)

Where:
- Trade_Link_ij = Trade relationship strength between countries i and j
- Decay = Impact decay per hop (typically 0.5-0.7)
- hop = Number of hops from source country
```

### 18.3 Network Graph Algorithm

```python
def propagate_event_impact(source_country, base_impact, trade_network):
    impacts = {source_country: base_impact}
    visited = set()
    queue = [(source_country, base_impact, 0)]
    
    while queue:
        country, impact, hops = queue.pop(0)
        if country in visited or hops > MAX_HOPS:
            continue
        visited.add(country)
        
        for neighbor, trade_weight in trade_network[country].items():
            propagated = impact * trade_weight * (DECAY ** hops)
            if propagated > MIN_IMPACT_THRESHOLD:
                impacts[neighbor] = impacts.get(neighbor, 0) + propagated
                queue.append((neighbor, propagated, hops + 1))
    
    return impacts
```

### 18.4 Multi-Hop Propagation Rules

| Hop | Decay Factor | Minimum Impact | Description |
|-----|--------------|----------------|-------------|
| 0 | 1.0 | N/A | Direct impact on source country |
| 1 | 0.6 | 1.0 | First-order trade partners |
| 2 | 0.36 | 0.5 | Second-order partners |
| 3 | 0.22 | 0.25 | Third-order partners |

### 18.5 Impact Decay Functions

```
Linear Decay: Impact(hop) = Base_Impact × (1 - hop × decay_rate)
Exponential Decay: Impact(hop) = Base_Impact × e^(-λ × hop)
Power Decay: Impact(hop) = Base_Impact × hop^(-α)
```

### 18.6 Trade Network Modeling

```python
def build_trade_network():
    # Data sources: IMF DOTS, UN Comtrade, World Bank
    network = {}
    for country in COUNTRIES:
        exports = get_export_data(country)
        imports = get_import_data(country)
        
        # Calculate trade intensity
        network[country] = {}
        for partner in COUNTRIES:
            if partner != country:
                trade_volume = exports[partner] + imports[partner]
                gdp = get_gdp(country)
                intensity = trade_volume / gdp
                if intensity > MIN_TRADE_THRESHOLD:
                    network[country][partner] = intensity
    
    return network
```
